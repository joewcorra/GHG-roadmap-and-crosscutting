Building a Modern U.S. GHG Inventory Data System

Maintaining Continuity, Embracing Opportunity

Our Challenge and Opportunity

With the discontinuation of the official EPA inventory compilation, we face the challenge of maintaining continuity in U.S. emissions tracking while seizing the opportunity to rebuild our system using modern practices that can serve the climate community for decades.

As your data system developer, my role is to create infrastructure that empowers each sector lead to do their best work while ensuring the overall inventory is coherent, defensible, and maintainable.

Four Guiding Principles

🡆 Simplicity First
Minimize Complexity, Maximize Utility

Adopt only solutions that make compilation easier, faster, more accurate, or more transparent. Every new feature must answer: Does this directly serve our mission?

👥 User-Centered Design
Learn from the Experts

Built for you, not imposed on you. Your domain expertise will guide all technical implementation decisions.

🔄 Iterative Development
Build, Learn, Improve

Start minimal, deploy quickly, gather feedback continuously, and improve based on real-world use.

🛡️ Transparency & Reproducibility
Building Trust Through Openness

Every step of data transformation, aggregation, and calculation will be documented and traceable.

Development Roadmap

Phase 1 Discovery (Weeks 1–4) — Meet with sector leads, document workflows, identify priorities

Phase 2 MVP (Months 2–4) — Build pilot sector, establish patterns, deliver working results

Phase 3 Expansion (Months 5–12) — Extend to additional sectors, refine based on lessons learned

Phase 4 Operations (Ongoing) — Maintain, improve, adapt, and ensure long-term sustainability

Technical Approach: Start Simple

🗄️ Our Foundation
R + tidyverse for calculations and analysis
Organized CSVs for transparent data storage
Git for version control and collaboration
Clear documentation for reproducibility
🎯 Why Simple Wins
Your data doesn’t need complex databases
Simple joins (2–4 tables) work great with R’s tidyverse tools
CSVs are transparent and git-friendly
Add complexity only when simple becomes painful

Key Insight: We’re optimizing for maintainability and transparency, not technological sophistication. Modern R handles your data volumes easily, and CSV files ensure that anyone can inspect, verify, and trust our inputs.
